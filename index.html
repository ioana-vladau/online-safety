Learn how to protect yourself online

Choose a respectable antivirus

A good way to protect your computer would be to choose a respectable antivirus or anti spyware software, that protects you from viruses specifically designed to spy on your activity.

Be careful what you download

Viruses can get through infected email attachments or digital file delivery companies, suspicious websites and USB connected devices.

Do not install two antivirus programs - use one at a time!

Be even more careful when downloading .exe files

.exe files are executable files that can contain viruses that can steal your data, damage it or encrypt it for a ransom.

Cover your webcam when not in use

S is for Safe. Look out for links without the “httpS://“

When scanning for viruses, your antimalware software will protect your email, monitor your files and scan USB devices ensuring that no spyware attacks your computer.


When on the internet roll your mouse pointer over a link to reveal its destination. Do not type personal information if the link is not secure: look out for links without the “https://“ before them. If you do not trust the website source, do a quick online search to identify if it is fraudulent or not.

Keep your e-mail safe

It is of the utmost importance to keep your email safe. Not only because it is most likely connected to your personal accounts but also because it can be used against you if you download an infected/disrupted file.


Do not open unsolicited junk email

To keep you safe, do not open unsolicited junk email such as hoax virus warnings, hoax charity appeals, chain email (often requesting you to forward it to your other contacts) and advertising.


Do not open emails if you do not know the sender

Do not use names in your password

Writing down your password on your computer or choosing your pet’s name as one is not a good idea. Instead, create a strong password by choosing a few random words with numbers and symbols.


Use both capital and lower case letters

To make it harder for hackers to break use a combination of capital and and lower case letters.


At least 8 characters

Choose at least eight characters! Do not enter your password when others can see what you are typing.


Use different passwords for different websites

Use different passwords for different websites. Do not share your passwords with anyone and change them often.

Use a password manager

If you can not memorize all your passwords you can choose a reputable password manager and store them there.

When shopping online make sure to keep the following advices in mind: Spot an unsecure site and stay away from it:


if it is not a “https://“ site, do not risk having your bank details stolen. Shop only from reputable shops: high-street or well established stores. When in doubt look up the stores details online. When shopping use a device that belongs to you on a trusted network.

Find out what sort of information social media networks, your browser and other companies know about you. This is your data footprint.
